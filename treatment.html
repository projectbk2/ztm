Treatment Options

Although many cases of thoracic outlet syndrome (TOS) can't be prevented, the condition is treatable. If left untreated, TOS can cause complications, such as: Permanent arm swelling and pain (especially in patients with venous TOS)


  1. Physical therapy is typically the first treatment and effective with manual treatment



  2. Botulinum toxin injections are sometimes effective when physical therapy doesn't completely relieve symptoms



  3. If symptoms persist after physical therapy and injections, surgery may be recommended
